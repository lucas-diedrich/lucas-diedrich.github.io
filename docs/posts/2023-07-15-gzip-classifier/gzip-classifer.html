Gzip is all you need? - Cell type annotation with a meme algorithm

Introduction

Recently, this tweet, talking about this very creative and interesting paper (Jiang et al. 2023) blew up on twitter. To keep it short, the authors demonstrated that a simple .gzip compression of text-based data, in combination with a kNN classifier performed as well on text classification tasks as state-of-the-art natural language models (here: BERT). Especially in cases of small to medium-sized datasets (where the training data does not suffice to sufficiently fine tune large language models), the minimalistic algorithm (XXX lines of python code ) performed extremely well. Intriguingly, it does not use any tuning parameters. This is of course somewhat embarassing1 for our multi-million dollar, Big Tech-backed deep learning models, trained on millions of GPU hours. Probs to the authors!

In recent history, the life sciences have somewhat blindly followed the latest deep-learning hype (to be fair - very often with great success), by transferring their favorite model type onto a biological question of choice (see Table 1), typically protein design or -omics.

Of course, this has also been the case for our latest hype algorithm!!! The paper was almost immediately adapted to the prediction of molecular properties of small molecules (Probst (2023), see Github and Twitter). This raises the question - can we throw also our latest hype algorithm on omics data?
